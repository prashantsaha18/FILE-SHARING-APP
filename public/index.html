Network Drive

Mount NexDrop as a Network Drive

NexDrop supports the standard **WebDAV** protocol. You can access and manage your files directly from your computer's native file explorer (Windows Explorer, macOS Finder, or Linux Files) just like a local USB drive.

Your Personal WebDAV URL http://localhost:3000/webdav

How to Mount on Your OS

  1. Open This PC in Windows File Explorer.
  2. Click Computer in the top ribbon and select Map network drive.
  3. Select a drive letter of your choice (e.g., Z:).
  4. In the Folder input field, paste your WebDAV URL shown above.
  5. Check both Reconnect at sign-in and Connect using different credentials, then click Finish.
  6. When prompted, enter your NexDrop **username** and **password**.
  7. Click OK. Your NexDrop files will now appear as a new drive under "This PC"!
  1. Open Finder on your Mac.
  2. Press Cmd + K on your keyboard (or click Go > Connect to Server... in the top menu bar).
  3. In the Server Address box, paste your WebDAV URL shown above.
  4. Click the Connect button.
  5. Select Registered User.
  6. Enter your NexDrop **username** and **password**, then click Connect.
  7. Your storage will now mount on your Desktop and inside Finder under Locations!
  1. Open your desktop file manager (e.g., GNOME Files / Nautilus).
  2. Click + Other Locations at the bottom of the sidebar.
  3. In the Connect to Server box at the bottom, enter your URL, but replace `http://` with `dav://` (or `https://` with `davs://`):
    dav://localhost:3000/webdav
  4. Click Connect.
  5. Select **Registered User**, enter your NexDrop **username** and **password**, and click **Connect**.
